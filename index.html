Standard Protocol v3.0
Severe Burns Management TRAUMA

1. Cool Immediately: Place affected area under clean, cool running tap water for at least 10–20 minutes. Do not use ice or ice-water.

2. Protect Membrane: Remove rings, jewelry, or restrictive clothing carefully before structural swelling sets in. Do not pop fluid blisters.

3. Loose Dressing: Cover loosely with a clean, sterile, non-stick gauze wrap or clear plastic kitchen cling film. Never apply butter or greasy ointments.

Arterial Hemorrhage Control TRAUMA

1. Direct Continuous Force: Apply heavy, firm, uninterrupted manual pressure directly onto the wound using sterile gauze pads or a clean cloth clothing barrier.

2. Elevate Limb: Maintain structural pressure while lifting the bleeding limb above heart level to limit vascular pressure vectors.

3. Tourniquet Deployment: If severe limb bleeding threatens life and direct pressure fails, apply a tactical tourniquet 2-3 inches above the wound area.

Fractures & Dislocations TRAUMA

1. Absolute Immobilization: Keep the affected bone structure completely still. Do not attempt to force, snap, or reset structural joints back into position.

2. Structural Splinting: Apply a rigid external object (boards, rolled newspapers) above and below the fracture node to restrict joint torque.

3. Cold Therapy: Apply enclosed ice packs wrapped inside a cloth layer to minimize subdermal swelling. Do not apply ice directly to skin.

Cardiopulmonary CPR Framework CARDIAC

1. Ground Placement: Position the patient flat on their back over a firm, unyielding surface. Position yourself directly adjacent to their chest line.

2. Continuous Compressions: Interlock fingers, place the heel of your primary hand on the center lower sternum bone, and compress down 2 to 2.4 inches.

3. Target Rate: Execute compressions at a steady speed of 100 to 120 beats per minute. Allow full chest recoil between actions.

Choking Emergency (Heimlich) AIRWAY

1. Verify Choking Vector: Confirm if the individual can speak, gasp, or forcefully cough. If silent, immediate intervention is mandatory.

2. Hand Fist Placement: Move behind the victim, wrap your arms around their waistline, and place a thumb-side closed fist slightly above the navel indicator.

3. Inward Upward Thrusts: Grasp your fist firmly with your opposite hand and deliver rapid, powerful, inward-and-upward structural thrusts until clear.

Stroke Protocol (F.A.S.T.) CARDIAC

F - Facial Asymmetry: Ask the person to smile. Monitor if one side of their lips drifts downward or feels completely numb.

A - Arm Drift Weakness: Instruct them to extend both arms horizontally forward. Check if one arm tracking shifts down involuntarily.

S - Speech Impediment: Ask them to repeat a simple sentence structure. Listen for distorted, slurred, or incoherent syllables.

T - Time Metric Emergency: If any symptoms align, record the exact onset timestamp and call emergency response immediately.

Venomous Snake Bite Stasis ENVIR

1. Total Physical Stasis: Keep the patient absolutely calm and stationary. Physical exertion speeds up systemic venom distribution.

2. Neutral Limb Tracking: Clean the bite area gently, remove restrictive clothing bands, and maintain the affected limb below or level with the heart line.

3. Prohibited Actions: Do not cut across the fang incisions, do not apply a suction device, and do not place ice over the tissue.

Chemical Toxin & Poison Ingestion ENVIR

1. Identify Substance Node: Isolate the container or compound if safe to inspect. Do not induce vomiting unless directly instructed by medical control.

2. Corrosive Management: For external chemical contacts, immediately flush skin tissue surfaces with clear running water for 15-20 minutes.
